Data Retention and Deletion Policies

How Relancy manages the lifecycle of operational data.

Back to Knowledge Base

Overview

Relancy maintains strict data retention and deletion policies to support compliance, audit integrity, and operational continuity. These policies ensure that data is preserved for as long as required and removed safely when appropriate.

Retention principles

  • Audit-first: Data required for audit or compliance is retained.
  • Tenant-specific: Retention policies apply per tenant.
  • Immutable lineage: Historical records cannot be altered.

Retention periods

Default retention periods vary by entity type. Examples include:

  • Cases and incidents: retained for the life of the tenant
  • Investigations: retained for the life of the tenant
  • Training lineage: retained indefinitely
  • System logs: retained according to platform policy

Deletion policies

Deletion is restricted to ensure audit integrity. When deletion is permitted:

  • It is logged in the audit trail
  • It does not remove historical lineage
  • It does not break lifecycle records

Tenant offboarding

Upon tenant offboarding, data is securely deleted following a structured, irreversible process. Backups associated with the tenant are purged according to retention schedules.
