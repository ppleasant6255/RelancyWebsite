Reopening Overview

Cases may be reopened when new evidence emerges, procedural errors are identified, or corrective actions fail. Reopening is a formal, auditable event.

Reopening criteria

  • New evidence or witness statements
  • Incorrect or incomplete findings
  • Procedural or compliance errors
  • Corrective action failure
  • Leadership or legal request

Operational safeguards

Reopening requires justification and elevated approval. Original findings remain preserved for audit purposes.

Audit integrity

Reopening events include timestamp, actor, justification, and references to new evidence or procedural issues.
