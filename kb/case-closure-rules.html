Case Closure Rules

How Relancy governs case closure using structured criteria, verification requirements, and audit safeguards.

Closure Overview

Case closure requires all investigative steps, interviews, evidence reviews, and corrective actions to be completed and verified. Closure is a formal, auditable event.

Closure criteria

  • All interviews completed
  • All evidence reviewed and classified
  • Findings documented and approved
  • Corrective actions completed and verified
  • No outstanding conflicts or escalations

Operational safeguards

Cases cannot be closed by the same investigator who performed verification. Sensitive cases require elevated approval.

Audit integrity

Closure events include timestamp, actor, justification, and a snapshot of case status for defensibility.
