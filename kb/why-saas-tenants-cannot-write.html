Why SaaS Tenants Cannot Write Into the Platform

Protecting audit integrity, lifecycle discipline, and tenant isolation.

Back to Knowledge Base

Overview

Relancy’s SaaS tier is designed to be safe, predictable, and audit-first. To preserve these guarantees, tenants in the shared SaaS environment cannot write custom logic into the platform, bypass lifecycle events, or directly mutate core entities through custom code or APIs.

This article explains why that boundary exists and how it protects both you and the platform.

Audit integrity

Relancy maintains a complete, tamper-resistant record of what happened, when it happened, and who was involved. Allowing tenants to inject custom logic or write directly into core entities would:

  • Introduce inconsistent behavior across tenants
  • Make audit trails harder to trust and interpret
  • Increase the risk of accidental or intentional data manipulation

By keeping write-level control inside the platform’s own lifecycle and rules, Relancy ensures that every action is traceable, explainable, and consistent.

Lifecycle discipline

Cases, incidents, investigations, and training all follow defined lifecycle stages. These stages are not cosmetic—they are the backbone of how Relancy measures readiness, risk, and response.

If tenants could write directly into the platform or override lifecycle logic, they could:

  • Skip required stages
  • Bypass approvals or reviews
  • Alter the meaning of lifecycle states
  • Break downstream reporting and analytics

To prevent this, lifecycle transitions are controlled by the platform, not by tenant-authored code.

Tenant isolation and safety

In a multi-tenant SaaS environment, one tenant’s behavior must never affect another tenant’s safety, performance, or data. Allowing arbitrary write-level access or custom logic would:

  • Increase the risk of performance degradation from poorly designed integrations
  • Complicate incident response and troubleshooting
  • Expand the attack surface for security vulnerabilities

By limiting tenants to safe, read-only and event-driven surfaces, Relancy keeps the shared platform stable and predictable for everyone.

What SaaS tenants can do instead

Even without write-level access, SaaS tenants still have powerful ways to integrate and extend Relancy:

  • Receive webhooks for key lifecycle events
  • Use reporting and export capabilities to feed BI tools
  • Add custom fields and panels to capture tenant-specific context
  • Build external automations that react to Relancy events

These options provide flexibility without compromising platform integrity.

When write-level access is appropriate

Organizations that truly require write-level access, custom logic, or dedicated infrastructure can use Relancy’s Private Deployment tier. In that model, you operate in an isolated environment with your own API cluster, database, and scaling rules—and you assume the corresponding control and responsibility.

In other words: write-level access belongs in Private Deployment, not in shared SaaS.
