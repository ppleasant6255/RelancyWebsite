Incident Reporting Best Practices

How to ensure accurate, timely, and actionable incident reports.

Back to Knowledge Base

Overview

Effective incident reporting is essential for safety, compliance, and operational awareness. Relancy provides structured tools to help teams capture accurate information quickly and consistently.

Key principles

  • Timeliness: Report incidents as soon as possible.
  • Accuracy: Record facts, not assumptions.
  • Completeness: Include all relevant details.
  • Neutrality: Use objective, professional language.

Required information

Incident reports should include:

  • Date and time of the event
  • Location
  • People involved
  • Description of what occurred
  • Immediate actions taken
  • Any supporting evidence

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Speculation or personal opinions
  • Incomplete timelines
  • Missing witness information
  • Unclear or ambiguous descriptions

Audit and follow‑up

All incident reports are logged in the audit trail and may trigger lifecycle transitions, investigations, or training assignments depending on severity.
