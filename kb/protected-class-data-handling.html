Protected-Class Data Handling

How Relancy safeguards sensitive demographic and legally protected information.

Back to Knowledge Base

Overview

Relancy is designed to handle sensitive operational data, including information related to protected classes. This article provides a deeper look at how the platform safeguards this information and enforces strict privacy boundaries.

What counts as protected-class data

Protected-class data includes attributes such as:

  • Race and ethnicity
  • Gender and gender identity
  • Age
  • Disability status
  • Veteran status
  • Religion
  • Sexual orientation
  • Other categories defined by law or policy

Data minimization

Relancy collects only the minimum protected-class information required for operational workflows. Sensitive fields are restricted, encrypted, and excluded from general reporting unless explicitly required.

Access control

Access to protected-class data is governed by:

  • Role-based permissions
  • Need-to-know access
  • Audit logging of all access attempts

Audit and traceability

Every interaction with protected-class data is logged with:

  • Timestamp
  • Actor
  • Action
  • Context

Isolation and storage

Protected-class data is isolated per tenant and encrypted at rest. It is never co-mingled with other tenants’ data.
