Case Merge and Split Rules

How Relancy handles combining related cases or separating multi‑topic cases into distinct workflows.

Back to Knowledge Base

Overview

Case merging and splitting help organizations maintain clarity and accuracy in case management. Relancy provides structured rules to ensure these actions are performed safely and with full audit traceability.

When to merge cases

  • Multiple cases involve the same individuals
  • Incidents share a common root cause
  • Duplicate cases were created
  • Investigations overlap significantly

When to split cases

  • A case contains multiple unrelated issues
  • Separate investigations are required
  • Different departments must handle different aspects

Audit and integrity

All merges and splits are logged with timestamp, actor, and justification. Historical relationships remain visible for compliance and review.

Restrictions

Sensitive or protected‑class cases may require elevated permissions to merge or split.
