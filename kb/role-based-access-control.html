Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

How Relancy enforces permissions and access boundaries across HR, Security, and leadership roles.

Back to Knowledge Base

Overview

Relancy uses a role-based access control model to ensure that users can access only the information and actions appropriate to their responsibilities. RBAC protects sensitive data, enforces privacy boundaries, and supports operational clarity.

Core roles

Common roles include:

  • HR Administrator: Full access to employee records and HR cases.
  • Security Officer: Access to incidents, investigations, and risk signals.
  • Manager: Limited access to direct reports and readiness data.
  • Investigator: Access to assigned investigations and evidence.
  • Training Coordinator: Access to training lineage and readiness dashboards.

Permission categories

Permissions are grouped into categories such as:

  • View-only access
  • Case creation and assignment
  • Investigation management
  • Training administration
  • Reporting and exports
  • Protected-class access

Least privilege principle

Relancy enforces least privilege by default. Users receive only the permissions required for their role.

Audit logging

All access to sensitive data is logged, including attempts to access restricted information.
