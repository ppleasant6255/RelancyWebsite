Custom Fields vs Custom Logic

How to extend Relancy safely without changing platform behavior.

Back to Knowledge Base

Overview

Relancy allows tenants to adapt the platform to their context through custom fields and panels, but does not allow tenant-authored logic in the shared SaaS environment. This distinction is intentional: it preserves audit integrity, lifecycle consistency, and tenant isolation while still giving you room to capture what matters locally.

Custom fields: data, not behavior

Custom fields let you store additional information on entities such as cases, incidents, or people. They are:

  • Tenant-specific
  • Data-only
  • Non-executable
  • Included in reporting and exports

Custom fields allow you to reflect your organization’s language, categories, and context without changing how the platform behaves.

Panels and presentation extensions

In addition to fields, Relancy can expose panels or sections that surface contextual information, links, or visualizations. These are presentation extensions, not logic hooks. They help your teams see more without giving external systems control over lifecycle or audit.

Custom logic: where it belongs

In the shared SaaS environment, custom logic is not supported. Allowing tenants to inject logic would:

  • Introduce inconsistent behavior across tenants
  • Complicate support and troubleshooting
  • Increase security and stability risks
  • Undermine lifecycle and audit guarantees

For organizations that truly need custom logic, Relancy offers a Private Deployment tier with dedicated infrastructure and optional write-level APIs.

Practical guidance

  • Use custom fields to capture additional attributes you need for reporting or context.
  • Use panels to surface external information without granting control to external systems.
  • Use webhooks and external automation to react to Relancy events in your own environment.
  • Consider Private Deployment if you require deep, platform-level customization.
