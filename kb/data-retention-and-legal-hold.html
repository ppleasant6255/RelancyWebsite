How Relancy enforces data retention schedules and legal hold requirements across all modules and storage layers.

Retention Overview

Data retention policies define how long incidents, cases, evidence, training records, and audit logs are stored. Legal holds override retention schedules to preserve data for litigation or regulatory review.

Retention categories

  • Incident and case records
  • Evidence and attachments
  • Training and readiness data
  • Audit logs and system events
  • Communication records

Operational safeguards

Data under legal hold cannot be deleted or modified. Retention changes require elevated approval and justification.

Audit integrity

Retention and legal hold events include timestamp, actor, affected records, and justification for compliance alignment.
