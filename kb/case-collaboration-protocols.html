Collaboration Overview

Cases frequently require input from multiple investigators, supervisors, and subject-matter experts. Relancy provides structured collaboration protocols that maintain data integrity, role boundaries, and audit trails across all participants.

Collaboration roles

  • Primary Investigator: owns the case and drives the investigation
  • Co-Investigator: contributes findings and evidence under the primary lead
  • Supervisor: reviews progress, approves key decisions, and manages escalations
  • Subject-Matter Expert: provides domain expertise on specific aspects of the case

Collaboration features

  • Real-time activity feed showing all participant actions
  • Shared notes with role-based visibility controls
  • Evidence contribution with chain-of-custody tracking per contributor
  • Task assignment and status tracking across collaborators
  • Handoff workflows when primary ownership changes

Access boundaries

  • Collaborators only see data permitted by their role and case assignment
  • Confidential and protected notes remain restricted regardless of collaboration status
  • Tenant isolation is enforced across all collaboration actions

Audit integrity

Every collaboration action, including joins, contributions, handoffs, and departures, is logged with full user, role, tenant, and timestamp context.
