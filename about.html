Built for the Real World
Guided by Investigative Discipline

Relancy is a governed investigative platform designed around operational reality, domain privacy, and director‑level defensibility. It exists because real investigations require structure, integrity, and clarity — not dashboards, summaries, or noise.

Our Mission
To bring investigative discipline, operational clarity, and defensible governance to organizations of every size.

Why Relancy Exists

Most platforms treat investigations as a workflow problem. Relancy treats them as a discipline — one that protects people, protects organizations, and protects the truth. The platform is built to reflect how real investigations work: incident‑first, domain‑private, escalation‑only, and fully auditable.

Guiding Principles
The platform is built on a set of non‑negotiable rules that shape every workflow.

Incident‑First

Cases cannot exist without incidents. No drafts, no placeholders, no pretext files.

  • Prevents retaliation and dossier‑building
  • Ensures defensible documentation
  • Mirrors real investigative discipline

Domain Privacy

Security and HR operate in separate domains with no cross‑visibility unless explicitly shared.

  • No accidental exposure
  • No shared dashboards
  • Escalation‑only sharing

Escalation Integrity

Directors see everything — but only when an investigator chooses to escalate.

  • No omniscience
  • No summaries or filters
  • Full version history included

Immutable Audit Trails

Every action is logged. Nothing is overwritten. Nothing is hidden.

  • Supports legal and compliance review
  • Protects investigators
  • Preserves organizational integrity
Continuity & Governance
Relancy is designed to outlive any individual — including its founder.

Built for Longevity

Relancy’s governance model ensures continuity at the corporate level. The platform is structured so that leadership transitions, organizational changes, or personnel turnover never compromise investigative integrity.

  • Director‑level oversight
  • Clear chain‑of‑command boundaries
  • Versioned, auditable, immutable records
